Fix the Teaching Board Diagram & Hand Animation
The current behavior of the Teaching Board needs to be improved.
1. Intelligent Diagram Generation
Do not generate a square, circle, or any random diagram for every question.
The AI must first analyze the question and determine whether a diagram is actually required.
Only generate a diagram when it genuinely helps explain the solution.
The diagram must match the subject and question type.
Examples:
Mathematics (Geometry): Draw the correct geometric figure (triangle, circle, polygon, graph, coordinate plane, etc.).
Physics: Draw the appropriate circuit, force diagram, ray diagram, motion graph, vectors, free-body diagram, or any relevant physics illustration.
Chemistry: Draw molecular structures, reaction diagrams, apparatus, laboratory setup, atomic models, or other required scientific diagrams.
Biology: Draw only the necessary biological structures or labeled diagrams.
Numerical Problems: Only include diagrams if they are required to solve or explain the problem. Otherwise, do not generate unnecessary graphics.
If the question does not require a diagram, do not display one at all.
2. Correct Diagram Placement
Diagrams must never overlap or mix with the explanation text.
Always leave sufficient vertical spacing before and after each diagram.
Display the diagram inside its own dedicated section or container.
Ensure diagrams are clean, centered, responsive, and easy to understand on both mobile and desktop.
Labels, arrows, formulas, and annotations should remain clear and readable.
3. Improve Layout
The explanation text and the diagram should be visually separated.
Maintain consistent margins and padding.
Prevent any clipping, overlapping, or content collision.
The overall presentation should look professional, clean, and easy to follow.
4. Fix Hand Animation
The animated hand must always remain below the content being written, never above or on top of the text.
As the AI writes, the hand should follow underneath the writing line, just like a real hand writing on a board.
The hand must never cover, block, or hide any text, formulas, symbols, diagrams, or explanations.
The writing animation should remain smooth and realistic while keeping 100% of the content fully visible at all times.
Expected Result: The Teaching Board should generate only context-aware diagrams, place them in a separate and well-spaced section, and display the writing hand below the content so that no text, formulas, or diagrams are ever hidden during the animation.
